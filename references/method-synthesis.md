# Generalized Method Synthesis

## Rights boundary

This synthesis is an autonomous factory contract.
It does not redistribute decks, copy, marks, photographs, screenshots, product cutouts, illustrations, storyboard frames, fonts or editable client files.
All third-party material remains with its respective rights holder.
Use only the architecture, factory geometry, state logic, composition families and quality controls.
Acquire current, lawful materials independently for each new prospect and maintain a provenance ledger.

## Derivation boundary

The public package does not contain source-specific page counts, unique page sequences, copied coordinates, distinctive third-party motifs, rotations or defect lists tied to an individual dossier. A flattened export cannot prove exact fonts, source-image origin, licenses, masks or editable settings. The system below is a new factory contract, not a reconstruction recipe for another dossier.

## Confidence model

- **Measured:** geometry, page count, flat color roles, object position and exported pixel behavior.
- **Probable:** font class, intended state relationships and some master logic.
- **Interpretive:** strategic role, persuasion dependency and transfer lesson.

## Invariant persuasion machine

```text
cover idea
-> architecture
-> current moment
-> existing capital
-> visible gap or risk
-> strategic shifts
-> owned territory
-> central tension or method
-> platform rules
-> controlled variations
-> selected first activation
-> execution proof
-> production confidence
-> relevant prior proof
-> editorial close
-> silent lockup
```

The dependency matters more than page number:

This is not a diagram-first sequence. In the Black Flower profile, alternate abstraction with visible reality. Current communication, product, archive, person, material, cultural reference, campaign scene and storyboard carry the argument at least as often as text. Use 45–65% image-led pages, target 55%, and no more than two true diagrams.

1. Evidence precedes critique.
2. Critique precedes strategic reframe.
3. Reframe precedes platform.
4. Platform precedes routes or applications.
5. One selected proof precedes production credentials.
6. Editorial close precedes silent lockup.

## Four-act operating model

### Act I: earn the right to propose

- Establish a verified current moment, or a durable observed tension when no time-bound event exists.
- Recognize what already works.
- Show current communication, product or technical proof.
- Name the perception gap or category shortcut fairly.
- Formulate two or three actionable shifts.

### Act II: make the angle ownable

- Identify one distinctive company truth.
- Connect it to a human, cultural or business tension.
- State territory and promise.
- Condense the territory into a short internal platform.
- Define viewpoint, code, territory and tone.

### Act III: prove range without fragmentation

- State the rule of variation.
- Apply one platform to products, audiences, tensions or formats.
- Use one repeated master so differences remain conceptual.
- Give each route a distinct situation, pressure, proof and implication.

### Act IV: descend into execution

- Select one first activation.
- Explain why this format, subject and moment.
- Show concept mechanics.
- Add storyboard, frames, prototype, timeline or channel proof.
- Introduce production role and relevant prior proof.
- End with an editorial close and a separate quiet lockup.

## Variable prospect grammars

| Model | Compatible background rhythm | Persistent object | Orientation detail |
|---|---|---|---|
| Symbol-led | `binary-chapter` | Cropped verified brand symbol | Generated chapter state plus eligible interior pagination |
| Product-led | `stable` | Simplified product-derived silhouette | Deterministic eligible interior progress marker |
| Type-led | `stable` or `binary-chapter` | Oversized letterform or numeral system | Generated typographic cadence |
| Grid-only | Neutral profile only | Modular rules and negative space | Not an admissible Black Flower default |

No prospect grammar is a default. Select one after identifying a real company object, field and orientation behavior. In Black Flower mode, its field behavior must still resolve to `stable` or `binary-chapter`, with only `cover` and `body` field assignments.

For Black Flower, prefer symbol-led, product-led or type-led systems with a concrete image cadence. Never select grid-only merely because the prospect is Swiss, technical or restrained.

## Page-count profiles

### Focused, 15 pages

Use 15 pages when one focused opportunity can still support a visual baseline, two distinct routes, an inspectable storyboard and Black Flower production presence. Never use compression as a reason to remove the visual proof that makes the proposal tangible.

Suggested allocation:

Cover; architecture and relevance; brand truth; current baseline; risk; opportunity; platform; product truth; two visual routes; priority activation; storyboard; production Black Flower; thank-you; lockup.

### Standard, 18-20 pages

Use when diagnosis and brand truth each deserve proof, the platform needs emotional and operational articulation, and one activation can be demonstrated concretely.

Target 9–12 image-led pages depending on whether the dossier contains 18, 19 or 20 pages. Reserve separate visual pages for current communication, product truth, routes, activation, storyboard and production.

### Expanded, 18-20 pages

Use only when explicitly requested or justified, three or four genuinely distinct routes exist, one route has a developed concept and storyboard, and the reader will assess a full campaign proposal.
Do not add pages only to resemble an expanded source.

## Typography and color rules

- Default to a disciplined sans serif system, then adapt it to verified brand guidance.
- Heavy uppercase titles dominate; body remains regular and editorial.
- Cover display may use a lighter weight; one system may add expressive numerals.
- Exact families are not confirmed by raster exports.
- Use official brand guidance first, otherwise document and test a substitute.
- Define color by role: main field, alternate field, main ink, inverse ink, object neutral, chapter accent, semantic accent and optional proof-only product color.
- Do not turn a photographic product color into a typographic token automatically.
- Test pale accents at real display size.
- Record chapter-state transitions before layout.

## Image-role synthesis

Every visual should have one of six jobs:

1. Current-state proof.
2. Distinctive company truth.
3. Risk shorthand.
4. Route or application scene.
5. Execution proof.
6. Production confidence.

Common treatment:

- one dominant visual per standard page;
- minimal radius;
- no heavy frame or shadow;
- documentary screenshots stay documentary;
- proof cutouts may overlap a scene;
- no generic luxury or technology filler;
- every visual receives a provenance and rights record.

## Content contract

Each page record must contain page ID, act, chapter, master, reader question, single claim, evidence IDs, title, subtitle, body blocks, image job, required material, source and rights, visual state, transitions and closing implication.

Rules:

- One dominant claim per page.
- Every fact has a source and access date.
- Every changing metric is rechecked before export.
- Fact, observation, inference and proposal remain distinct.
- Every route traces back to the platform.
- Every prior-work entry states relevance.
- Internal alternatives never reach client copy.
- Copy is edited before type is reduced.
- Production credentials appear after prospect-specific proof.

## Rights record

For every visual, record material ID, owner or source, creator, license or permission, access date, original dimensions, allowed edits, crop notes, retouch notes, page uses, credit requirement and approval status.

Prefer prospect-provided originals and official press materials for discovery quality, but treat origin and permission separately. Neither `provided` nor `official` proves the intended use is cleared.
Date current interfaces and social evidence.
Verify historical material and cultural references.
Preserve originals separately from derivatives.
Inspect cutout masks at 200%.
Never infer origin or rights from appearance.
No third-party reference material is reusable without its own documented clearance.

1. Duplicated opportunity copy.
2. Internal note left in final copy.
3. Two undecided concepts joined by a slash.
4. Runtime without per-frame timing.
5. Storyboard or moodboard too small to inspect.
6. Prior-work page without inspectable proof.
7. CTA label and destination mismatch.
8. Undated changing metric.
9. Unknown cultural-image rights.
10. Full justification creating word-space rivers.
11. Long title entering the protected motif zone.
12. Accent-token drift without meaning.
13. Pale accent used below safe display size.
14. Cutout scale inconsistency across a family.
15. Progress marker drifting from its generated sequence.
16. Production page appearing before strategic proof.
17. Close without an editorial signature, prospect object or complete cover disclosure.
18. Exact font claimed without source evidence.

## QA contract

### Structural gate

- Architecture matches delivered chapters.
- Proof precedes critique.
- Platform precedes routes.
- Selected activation follows the route system.
- Production follows prospect-specific proof.
- Thank-you and final lockup remain separate.

### Content gate

- Every page has one takeaway.
- Facts and proposals are distinguishable.
- No duplicate, filler, internal note or unresolved option remains.
- Company terminology and changing facts are verified.
- Next action matches how the dossier will be consumed.

### Visual gate

- Canvas, rails, margins and footer zones are consistent.
- Persistent object follows its declared state sequence.
- Progress is generated, not manually nudged.
- Essential text remains outside protected zones.
- Repeated media share size, crop and radius.
- Proof is readable at 100%; cutouts are clean at 200%.
- Contact-sheet pacing matches chapter logic.

### Link and rights gate

- Every URL opens and every QR decodes from the final render.
- Label and destination match.
- Every visual has a provenance record.
- Every permission covers the intended use.
- No original case material is bundled in the publishable layer.

## Generation algorithm

1. **Audit:** verify facts, inspect communication, inventory lawful materials, identify strengths, gap and category shortcut.
2. **Strategize:** extract a distinctive truth, connect it to an external tension, score directions, define viewpoint, code, territory and tone.
3. **Map:** derive visual roles, choose pagination, assign one takeaway, evidence set and image job per page, then write within master budgets.
4. **Produce:** acquire materials with provenance, implement repeated families, inspect contact sheet and details, then deliver source, exports, ledgers and QA report.

## Transfer contract

Always transfer argument dependencies, one idea per page, stable A-series geometry, proof before recommendation, company-derived architecture, functional color, platform before variations, selected proof before production, separate close and lockup, and evidence discipline.

Always rebuild palette, typography, material field, motif, progress behavior, language, images, platform, route logic, chapter codes, page count, marks, activation and next action.
