# Copy System

## Purpose

Write concise strategic copy that feels researched, specific and authored for the prospect. The writing should carry the argument before design is added.

## 1. Voice

Use a direct, concrete, quietly confident voice.

The dossier should sound like:

- an attentive reading of the company;
- a precise recommendation;
- an artisan proud of the work;
- a creative partner who respects existing capital;
- a proposal that can be discussed, not a proclamation of absolute truth.

It should not sound like:

- corporate consultancy;
- a marketing agency manifesto;
- a press release;
- a trend report;
- praise without evidence;
- an overfamiliar sales email.

Prefer concrete nouns and verbs: show, clarify, reveal, connect, organize, prove, guide, choose, read, build, preserve, transform, embody.

## 2. Language policy

### Default French output

- Use Swiss French.
- Address the client with `vous` when direct address is necessary.
- Use French punctuation and accents correctly.
- Never use em dashes in client-facing copy. Use a full stop, comma, colon or parentheses.
- Avoid region-specific French expressions that feel foreign in Switzerland.
- Avoid protected professional-title wording prohibited by the active project instructions.
- Keep a direct, sober and non-corporate tone.

### English inside a French dossier

English is acceptable for:

- a short platform phrase;
- a cover title;
- concise strategic labels such as `Why now?`, `The risk`, `The opportunity`;
- film or route names;
- one final signature.

Use English deliberately, not because a sentence sounds more premium. Explain the strategy in French unless the user's brief or the company's language system indicates otherwise.

### Consistency decision

Record one choice in `brief.yaml`:

- `fr-CH`: French copy, optional English platform;
- `en`: English throughout;
- `bilingual`: only when the prospect already communicates bilingually or the user requests it.

Do not mix languages within the same body paragraph.

### Quotes and translations

- Preserve exact wording, punctuation, attribution and source language for a verbatim quote.
- A translated quote is not verbatim. Label it `Traduction du studio` or paraphrase it as attributed analysis.
- Never back-translate a press line from memory and place it in quotation marks.
- Store the source wording and final client wording as separate claim-map rows when a translation is used.

## 3. Hierarchy and length budgets

These budgets prevent overflow and force clarity.

| Element | Ideal | Hard review threshold |
|---|---:|---:|
| Page title | 2–7 words | 72 characters or two lines |
| Subtitle | 6–18 words | 130 characters |
| Block title | 2–6 words | 45 characters |
| Standard body block | 30–55 words | 80 words |
| Three-column body | 25–45 words per column | 60 words per column |
| Quote or signature | 6–18 words | 28 words |
| Route description | 80–120 words total | 140 words |
| Reference entry | 30–55 words | 70 words |
| Thank-you letter | 75–135 words | 135 words |
| Image caption | 3–14 words | 24 words |

Crossing a threshold does not automatically fail. It requires either a deliberate smaller type treatment, a split page, or a rewrite. Do not silently shrink body copy below the visual-system range.

### One-page message test

For every page, write one sentence beginning with:

`After this page, the reader should believe...`

If the sentence contains `and` more than once, the page probably carries too many ideas.

## 4. Page-level formulas

### Cover

- prospect or product name;
- platform phrase;
- optional one-line signature;
- no explanatory paragraph.

The platform must carry the cover. Do not add generic descriptors such as `a strategic vision for tomorrow`.

### Architecture

Use four to six chapter labels. Each label must describe a movement in the argument, not a department.

Strong:

- Read the moment.
- Reveal the territory.
- Build the platform.
- Prove the first move.

Weak:

- Strategy.
- Content.
- Social media.
- Production.

### Why now?

Use:

1. `The moment`: what changed or is visible now.
2. `The challenge`: why current signals may not communicate the full reality.
3. `The opportunity`: what a clearer system can unlock.

Do not duplicate the moment in the opportunity. End each column with a distinct consequence.

### Existing foundation

Begin with respect. Identify three real strengths before recommending change.

Pattern:

- strength title;
- visible proof;
- why the strength matters.

Avoid empty praise such as `une marque iconique`, `un savoir-faire unique` or `une qualité exceptionnelle` without precise proof.

### Risk

Name the shortcut calmly. Use three levels when useful:

1. the obvious territory;
2. the creative shortcut;
3. the consequence for perception.

Do not dramatize with alarm language. The page should feel diagnostic, not accusatory.

### Opportunity

Use two or three shifts:

- from a current reading;
- to a more useful reading;
- one short explanation of what changes.

Keep the `from` respectful. The company is not being rescued by the proposal.

### Platform

Define:

- point of view;
- real brand or product code;
- territory;
- tone.

Each block should be independently useful when writing or judging future content.

### Manifesto

Write short sentences with deliberate rhythm. Use one preserved strategic idea, not a poem made of abstractions.

Useful sequence:

1. what the world or category does;
2. what becomes difficult;
3. what the brand makes possible;
4. three active verbs or behaviors;
5. platform signature.

### Communication system

Name each component by role, then state its output or proof. Avoid generic service labels.

Strong: `Visual proof. Show the people, place, process and product details that make capability tangible.`

Weak: `Content creation. Produce engaging content for all channels.`

### Routes

Each route needs:

- a specific name;
- tension;
- proposed scene or proof;
- brand code;
- final line.

The final lines should not be interchangeable.

### Priority activation

Answer explicitly:

- why this move;
- why this subject or product;
- why now.

Do not hide the recommendation inside descriptive prose.

### Film concept

Use three short movements such as:

- scene;
- pressure;
- signal or shift.

Write images and actions, not a production treatment full of adjectives.

### Production

Use three blocks:

- `Notre rôle`;
- `Notre approche`;
- `Notre force`.

Lead with what the studio will make clearer or more useful for the prospect. Keep credentials concise.
The approved isolated Black Flower portrait is mandatory. Do not replace it with a production still, reference proof or generic biography image.

### References

Every entry has:

1. project and deliverable;
2. one factual description;
3. `Pertinence [prospect]`: the exact transferable capability.

Do not use a reference only for prestige.

### Thank-you

Write a short letter:

1. frame the dossier as an initial strategic and creative reading;
2. restate the opportunity in the prospect's language;
3. state what the retained platform is designed to make clearer, more visible or more memorable;
4. express why this platform matters from Black Flower's perspective, without implying an existing relationship;
5. close with `Merci encore et à bientôt !` or a faithful language equivalent.

Do not introduce new strategy on this page.
Do not add a CTA, contact details, URL, booking instruction or sales next step.

### Final lockup

Use only the real prospect mark, `×` and the Black Flower flower in the foreground. The documented prospect-derived cover motif may remain in the background. Keep the relationship truthful through the cover disclosure and metadata: `Proposition indépendante pour [prospect], par Black Flower Creative House` for independent prospecting, or the agreed approved or commissioned wording. The silent `×` is valid only because that disclosure is already explicit. Studio identity is optional only in a neutral profile. Do not imply that the prospect commissioned, approved or partnered on the work.

Do not add a CTA, paragraph, URL wall or new tagline.

## 5. Strategic phrasing

### Observation versus claim

Use:

- `Votre communication actuelle met surtout en avant...`
- `Nous observons une base solide autour de...`
- `Notre lecture est que...`
- `L'opportunité consiste à...`
- `Nous proposons de passer de... à...`

Avoid:

- `La marque souffre de...` unless supported by clear evidence and appropriate tone;
- `Vos clients veulent...` without customer evidence;
- `Le marché exige...` without a source;
- `Vous devez...` when `L'enjeu est de...` is more accurate;
- attributing intentions to leadership without a statement.

### Respectful contrast

Good shifts preserve existing capital:

- `Ne pas abandonner la précision. La rendre plus lisible.`
- `Ne pas effacer l'héritage. Le transformer en méthode active.`
- `Ne pas produire davantage. Organiser des preuves cohérentes.`

Avoid framing the proposal as a total correction of a failed brand.

### Specificity test

Replace the prospect's name with a close competitor. If the sentence remains fully true, add evidence, product code, place, process, audience or behavior until it becomes specific.

## 6. Studio and reference copy

Keep studio copy secondary to the prospect's opportunity.

If no verified studio identity exists, omit studio copy, mark, contact and signature. Do not invent them or leave empty client-visible scaffolding.

Use:

- what the studio will clarify, reveal or produce;
- how strategy, image and execution connect;
- a small number of relevant capabilities;
- a real named person and role when verified;
- references selected for transfer value.

Avoid:

- `full-service` language;
- awards or claims without evidence;
- long service inventories;
- self-congratulation;
- vague promises of disruption, impact or excellence.

## 7. Editing passes

Run these passes in order.

### Pass 1: evidence

- Check every factual noun, date, name, product and quote against evidence IDs.
- Check every translated quote is visibly labelled as a translation or rewritten without quotation marks.
- Convert interpretations into clearly framed readings.
- Remove unsupported superlatives.
- Give every final substantive visible field a deliberate claim class. Never apply `proposal` to all fields as a blanket fallback.
- Verify each claim uses a slide-relative path such as `title`, `columns[0].body` or `metrics[0]`, never `slides[0].title`.
- Match claim text to the exact visible target after normalization. A metric claim contains its visible value and label.

### Pass 2: argument

- Check that every page advances the narrative.
- Remove repeated ideas.
- Verify that risk leads to opportunity and opportunity leads to platform.

### Pass 3: specificity

- Run the competitor substitution test.
- Add concrete brand codes and proof.
- Remove generic category language.

### Pass 4: compression

- Cut throat-clearing openings.
- Replace adjective stacks with one precise noun or verb.
- Keep three examples, not seven.
- Split only when the second page carries a different decision.

### Pass 5: language and tone

- Verify Swiss French, accents and `vous`.
- Remove em dashes from client-facing copy.
- Check English is deliberate.
- Remove protected title wording prohibited by project instructions.
- Remove corporate filler.

### Pass 6: production residue

Search for:

- `TODO`, `TBC`, `TBD`, `placeholder`, `lorem`, `[insert`, `à compléter`;
- parentheses that look like internal notes;
- two unresolved options joined by `/`;
- old client and product names;
- duplicate paragraphs;
- inconsistent capitalization and platform spelling.

Run the automated final placeholder scan after this editorial pass. Manual search does not replace validation, and a blocked token must be removed rather than hidden or excluded from the scanner.

## 8. Prohibited patterns

Reject or rewrite:

- `Dans un monde en constante évolution...`;
- `repousser les limites`;
- `réinventer les codes` without a specific code and action;
- `allier tradition et innovation`;
- `une expérience unique`;
- `au cœur de` used as filler;
- `plus qu'une...` as a generic opening;
- `là où X rencontre Y`;
- adjective stacks such as `audacieux, innovant, authentique et inspirant`;
- invented customer sentiment;
- agency jargon such as funnel, ecosystem, omnichannel or storytelling when plain language works;
- a CTA that sounds like a landing page;
- a thank-you page that repeats the entire dossier.
