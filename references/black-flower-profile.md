# Black Flower Profile

## Purpose

Define the persistent authorship, image cadence and editorial behavior of a Black Flower prospect dossier. This profile is the default for Alex's work. It is not a neutral theme and it is not a visual style pasted over every company.

The prospect provides the visible world: products, materials, people, places, archives, current communication, cultural tension and campaign possibilities. Black Flower provides the point of view, sequence, hierarchy, restraint and production credibility.

## Identity contract

Use these exact values:

```yaml
framework_profile: black-flower
studio: Black Flower Creative House
studio_signature: BlackFlower
running_header: Strategic creative campaign proposal · BlackFlower
generative_assets: authorized
generative_assets_authorization:
  status: explicitly-authorized
  authorized_by: Alex Houser
  reference: black-flower-owner-brief-2026-07-18#visuals
campaign_mode: campaign-platform
creative_route_count: 2
```

Rules:

- `Black Flower Creative House` is the canonical studio name.
- `BlackFlower` is the visible signature in eligible interior running headers and non-final compact attributions. The silent final uses the Black Flower flower, never the wordmark.
- `Black Flower` is acceptable in natural prose.
- Nexaia Studio is the technical maintainer of the open-source tooling. It must never appear as the visible author of a Black Flower prospect dossier.
- Do not publish or bundle a Black Flower mark unless its source and redistribution rights are recorded. Use the text signature when a cleared mark is unavailable.
- In independent prospecting, describe the relationship truthfully. Do not imply a commission, client relationship or endorsement.

## Core visual law

The order of importance is:

1. meaningful image or object;
2. specific title;
3. short strategic explanation;
4. prospect-derived motif or material cue;
5. quiet Black Flower authorship.

Every page needs a visual event, but a visual event is not automatically a diagram. It may be a photograph, product cutout, screenshot, archive, cultural reference, storyboard, drawing, typographic interruption, material crop or intentional field of space.

Never convert an unmade image into:

- a set of equal cards;
- four nodes connected by arrows;
- an orbit, signal, grid or abstract line;
- a severity meter or progress bar;
- a pill cloud;
- a dark central platform box;
- a miniature pseudo-interface;
- a numbered framework whose only purpose is to fill the canvas.

In final mode, missing visual material is a production failure. It is not an invitation to render a graphic fallback.

## Contact-sheet contract

For a dossier of 15–20 pages:

- 45–65% of pages are image-led;
- target 55%;
- 15 pages: 7–9 image-led pages;
- 18 pages: 9–11 image-led pages;
- 20 pages: 10–12 image-led pages;
- at least 60% of pages that actually render substantive slide media use real, official, documentary, licensed or supplied assets;
- generated images remain at or below 40% of those pages, regardless of declared visual intent;
- no more than two true diagram pages;
- no more than two text-led or system-led pages consecutively after the introduction;
- at least six distinct composition silhouettes;
- at least three visible peaks at contact-sheet size;
- no composition family appears more than three times outside an eligible film-route series;
- do not repeat the same family on adjacent pages, except inside one exact declared film-route series of 2–4 pages.

Count a page as image-led only when a photograph, product, screenshot, archive, illustration, cultural reference, campaign scene or storyboard is the main rhetorical anchor. Logos, motifs, textures, decorative SVGs and tiny thumbnails do not count.

## Visual-intent contract

Assign one mode and a written rationale to every page before implementation:

| Mode | Meaning | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| `image-led` | The image carries the central argument | proof, tension, territory, activation, storyboard, production |
| `image-supported` | Text leads, but concrete media is necessary proof | current state, archive, method, references |
| `typographic` | Type and intentional space are the visual event | cover, manifesto, transition, thank-you |
| `diagram` | A real relation, sequence or quantity requires a diagram | architecture or system, maximum two pages |

The rationale must answer why this mode is better than the other three for the page's single takeaway.

## Image hierarchy

Search in this order:

1. official products and macro details;
2. clean official product cutouts;
3. screenshots of current communication;
4. archives and origins;
5. making, gestures, hands and materials;
6. founders, collaborators, users and ambassadors with verified identity;
7. workshops, boutiques, fairs, events and places;
8. relevant artistic, cinematic, musical, sporting or vernacular references;
9. metaphorical objects or situations;
10. storyboards, drawings and technical illustration;
11. authorized generated campaign scenes.

Give each image one primary role:

- current-state proof;
- brand truth;
- product truth;
- origin;
- counterexample;
- cultural shorthand;
- tone;
- creative projection;
- activation;
- film shot;
- Production portrait.

Reject an image if another generic image could replace it without changing the argument.

## Generative imagery

Alex's owner brief authorizes generation for Black Flower creative projection, not evidence. Every dossier must repeat the explicit authorizer and durable reference in its authorization block. Selecting the Black Flower profile alone grants nothing.

Authorized uses:

- campaign scenes;
- film territories;
- scenes that cannot reasonably be photographed during prospecting;
- coherent moodboards;
- storyboard frames;
- material, lighting or casting explorations.

Forbidden uses:

- fake logos or brand marks;
- exact products, watches, tools, interfaces or packaging presented as real;
- employees, founders or clients presented as real;
- factories, boutiques, events or archives presented as documentary;
- fabricated writing, dials, labels, product parts or technical properties;
- any generated image used as proof of an existing claim.

When a real product must appear in a generated campaign world:

1. generate the scene without the product or with a clean reserved zone;
2. prepare the official product cutout separately;
3. composite the exact cutout into the scene;
4. preserve proportions, reflections and material truth;
5. record both sources in the asset ledger.

For a series, lock casting, focal length, camera height, light direction, grain, palette and aspect ratio. Reject beautiful but narratively empty output.

## Composition grammar

Use these exact engine values in `compositionFamily`:

| Engine value | Use |
|---|---|
| `silent-cover` | Cover manifesto with type, object or true image |
| `editorial-split` | Image and text split with clear hierarchy |
| `editorial-columns` | Open editorial sequence, never equal cards |
| `image-dominant` | One scene occupies the rhetorical foreground |
| `object-overlap` | Scene plus exact product cutout |
| `evidence-field` | Screenshots, archive, product or proof collage |
| `typographic-manifesto` | Deliberate type-led pause |
| `editorial-sequence` | Image-led method or film sequence |
| `storyboard-grid` | Inspectable storyboard frames |
| `reference-wall` | Asymmetric reference or moodboard field |
| `portrait-profile` | Mandatory Black Flower Production portrait |
| `diagrammatic-system` | Necessary diagram, maximum two pages |
| `closing-letter` | Editorial thank-you letter with prospect object |
| `lockup` | Silent co-branded close |

Across one dossier, use at least six families. Never repeat the same family on adjacent pages and never use one family more than three times outside the strict film-route exception below. An `editorial-sequence` must give every step its own inspectable image. An `object-overlap` product must remain an exact cutout with `fit: contain`, never a generated approximation.

### Repeated film-route master

Allow one contiguous block of 2–4 `film-concept` slides to share the same compatible composition family only when:

- `meta.creativeRouteCount` is an integer from 2 to 4;
- every `film-concept` slide in the dossier belongs to that one block;
- the block length exactly equals `meta.creativeRouteCount`;
- every route uses the same family;
- that family is compatible with `film-concept`.

The adjacency exemption applies only between two pages inside the block. A matching family immediately before or after it remains invalid. Count all uses outside the block normally and reject the fourth outside use. A fragmented block, mixed master, mismatched declaration or five-route run receives no exception.

### Cover manifesto

- very large title;
- prospect identification and truthful Black Flower authorship;
- one prospect-derived motif or strong object crop;
- optional true image, never a pseudo-visual fallback;
- restrained information count.

### Oversized object crop

- product, movement, material or industrial detail occupies 30–50% of the canvas;
- crop intentionally exits one edge;
- copy remains short and spatially connected to the object;
- useful for product truth, transition or platform reveal.

### Editorial split

- image occupies 35–45% of the page;
- text occupies the remaining field;
- alternate image side across the dossier;
- no floating card around either half;
- one visual and no more than three text blocks.

### Reference scene with product cutout

- scene or cultural reference occupies 45–65%;
- exact product cutout may overlap by 4–10% of canvas width;
- the overlap must make the campaign relation visible;
- no glow, fake shadow or distorted product;
- central grammar for creative routes.

### Proof collage

- two to four screenshots, archives, products or activations;
- one image is visibly dominant;
- avoid equal card grids;
- connect the items through crop, scale, sequence or annotation.

### Product center

- one exact product or range cutout at the center or an intentional off-axis anchor;
- two to four short annotations only;
- no feature-dashboard treatment;
- useful when material or construction is the argument.

### Cultural metaphor

- one surprising reference that makes a precise strategic argument memorable;
- the metaphor must be explainable in one sentence;
- verify rights and replacement path;
- keep the tone respectful toward the prospect and audience.

### Film sequence

- three to six frames with readable progression;
- consistent aspect ratio and visual continuity;
- show action, not six decorative moods;
- text is limited to route, action, sound or timing.

### Territory moodboard

- material, light, casting, location and gesture;
- at least 35% visual surface;
- two to four elements with an intentional hierarchy;
- route-specific, not a generic luxury moodboard.

### Storyboard

- three to twelve real local frames;
- frames occupy at least 45% of the page;
- shot, action, sound and timing remain inspectable;
- no text-only storyboard in final mode.

### Production portrait

- real, approved, isolated vertical portrait;
- exact name, role and relevance;
- exact title `PRODUCTION BLACKFLOWER`;
- one-line prospect signature;
- exactly `Notre rôle`, `Notre approche` and `Notre force`;
- no workstreams, deliverables, constraints or dashboard residue;
- `fit: contain`, declared source dimensions and a validated subject safe box.

Use the complete measured contract in `black-flower-finishing.md`.

### Typographic manifesto

- one large statement and intentional space;
- maximum two such pages in the dossier;
- no empty field compensated by tiny labels or abstract decoration.

### Necessary diagram

- use only when an actual relationship, progression or quantity cannot be understood as clearly with an image and a sentence;
- maximum two pages;
- derive line, symbol and color behavior from the prospect;
- never use as the main visual for risk, creative route, activation or production.

## Typography and chrome

At 2000 × 1414 px:

| Role | Range |
|---|---:|
| Running header | 20–26 px |
| Page title | 58–82 px |
| Cover title | 72–118 px |
| Subtitle | 25–34 px |
| Block title | 32–46 px |
| Body | 24–32 px |
| Caption | 18–22 px |
| Page number | 32–44 px |

Rules:

- show the running header only on eligible interior pages and anchor it at top left: `Strategic creative campaign proposal · BlackFlower`;
- show the page number only on eligible interior pages and anchor it at bottom left;
- hide running header, page number, footer and chapter chrome on the cover and silent final;
- reserve the bottom-right marker for a prospect-derived chapter or progress cue when it adds value;
- use a sans-serif body family and optionally one expressive display family;
- use no more than four roles and three weights;
- reserve monospaced type for data, timecodes or technical evidence;
- do not use monospaced labels as the personality of every page;
- apply accent color to a few meaningful words, not every navigation element;
- keep essential body copy at 24 px or more;
- page title and main visual must remain legible in the contact sheet at 25%.

## Motif and material

The motif must be traceable to a real prospect object: product silhouette, handle, dial, bracelet, key, wafer, movement, tool, material section, package or architectural detail.

- one master motif and at most two states;
- crop across 20–45% of a side when used structurally;
- ghost state at roughly 4–14% opacity;
- full state on cover, true chapter peak or behind the silent final co-mark as part of the recurring cover field;
- use on roughly 40–70% of pages, not automatically on every page;
- be able to explain the origin in one sentence;
- never reduce it to a small repeated dashboard graphic.

Use paper grain, archive imperfection or material texture only when it belongs to the prospect's world. Keep texture around 2–5% intensity. Do not place images inside floating cards or add generic shadows, glows, marble, neon, smoke or luxury effects.

## Narrative image rhythm

The recurring rhythm is:

1. state an abstraction;
2. prove or dramatize it through something concrete;
3. introduce the platform;
4. show its visual world immediately;
5. deploy distinct routes with a repeatable scene-plus-product grammar;
6. descend into activation, moodboard, storyboard and Black Flower production.

A standard 18–20-page sequence may use:

1. cover;
2. architecture;
3. why now;
4. archive or brand truth;
5. current communication with screenshots;
6. risk with a cultural or documentary image;
7. opportunity;
8. platform;
9. product truth, material or gesture;
10. creative system;
11. product or range image;
12. route one;
13. route two;
14. route three;
15. priority activation or place;
16. execution moodboard;
17. storyboard;
18. closing chain: production Black Flower, optional standalone references, editorial thank-you, silent lockup.

The list expresses order, not one page per line. Compress or omit earlier optional beats to remain within 15–20 pages. For 15 pages, merge architecture and compatible proof pages, then omit References if necessary. Never merge Production, Merci or the final lockup. Do not remove the current-state baseline, visual routes, storyboard or Black Flower production presence when the brief includes campaign execution.

## Automatic rejection patterns

Reject the dossier when it contains:

- dashboard slides;
- repeated grids of three or four equal cards;
- decorative flowcharts;
- timelines without substantive images;
- invented KPIs or pseudo-data;
- decorative arrows, nodes, badges or progress bars;
- corporate isometry;
- glassmorphism, neon, smoke, black marble, gold dust or floating-product clichés;
- interchangeable stock photography;
- more than two consecutive text/system pages after the introduction;
- a motif with no prospect-derived origin;
- generated anatomy, writing, logo or product errors;
- a visual with no argumentative role;
- repeated crops or page silhouettes;
- cover or closing signed Nexaia;
- a dossier that still works after replacing only the logo.

## Final visual audit

Run two kill tests.

First, hide every logo and ask:

> Can the prospect be recognized through the visible objects, materials, people, references and places?

If not, the dossier is not specific enough.

Second, view only the contact sheet:

- at least half the thumbnails show concrete visual material;
- at least six composition silhouettes are visible;
- three pages stop the eye immediately;
- no long tunnel of tables, cards or diagrams exists;
- the platform is followed by a visual world, not another framework page;
- routes, activation, storyboard and production are visually inspectable.

Fail the dossier if either kill test fails, even when type-check, build and PDF export succeed.
